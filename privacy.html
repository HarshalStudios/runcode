Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 6, 2026

1. Ownership and Workspace Files

At RunCode, we ensure that your computer science work, source files, and workspace files remain completely private, secure, and under your ownership. We do not read, upload, analyze, or transmit your private files or source code to our servers. All code files you create, edit, or compile in Python, Java, C, C++, JavaScript, HTML, or CSS are stored completely locally on your Android device.

  • Storage Access Framework (SAF): RunCode strictly utilizes Android's system SAF. We only access files or folders that you explicitly grant permission to within the system file chooser.
  • No File Scanning: We do not scan your private files or directory structures for unauthorized purposes.
  • Ownership: You own 100% of the code, scripts, projects, and educational files you write using RunCode.

2. Third-Party Infrastructure and Analytics

To maintain high compiler speed and stability, we integrate standard Google/Firebase libraries inside the Android application package:

  • Firebase Analytics & Crashlytics: We collect anonymous telemetry logs, build performance timings, and application crash reports to debug interpreter exceptions and compiler failures.
  • Google AdMob: We serve minimal, non-intrusive advertisements to help support free educational compilation. These comply with Google Play Family and student privacy standards.
  • Google Play Billing: Processes standard purchases and renewals securely for our Premium Student tier and ad-free upgrades. No credit card information is seen or stored by us.

3. No Selling of Data

We will never sell, distribute, rent, or lease your personal metadata, source code, or telemetry logs to any third-party brokers, advertisers, or organizations. All diagnostic data collected via Google Services is used strictly for debugging app performance.
